Here is a summary of the clinical note:

**Patient Information:**

* The patient is a female with a carotid body tumor.
* She has a history of hypertension, depression, dizziness, diabetes, high lipid levels, arthritis, obesity, hip/lower leg fracture, and R wrist fracture.
* She has undergone gastric bypass surgery and repair of left hip/lower leg and right arm.

**Chief Complaint and History of Present Illness:**

* The patient presented with a 3-cm right carotid body tumor and underwent coil embolization of the feeding external carotid branches before being admitted for resection.

**Surgical Intervention:**

* The patient underwent excision of the right carotid body tumor with preservation of the internal and external carotid arteries and facial nerve monitoring.

**Post-Operative Course:**

* The patient did well without any complications, including groin swelling or facial or neuro deficits.
* She had a right JP drain below her incision to monitor drainage, which was removed on post-operative day 4.
* She was able to tolerate a regular diet, get out of bed, and ambulate without assistance, and her pain was controlled on oral medications alone.

**Medications:**

* The patient was taking various medications on admission, including fluticasone propionate, gabapentin, metformin, omeprazole, pravastatin, ranitidine, sucralfate, aspirin, vitamin D, cyanocobalamin, and multivitamins.
* She was discharged on a regimen of aspirin, metformin, omeprazole, ranitidine, sucralfate, acetaminophen, docusate sodium, oxycondone, senna, cyanocobalamin, fluticasone propionate, gabapentin, multivitamins, pravastatin, and vitamin D.

**Discharge Disposition and Instructions:**

* The patient was discharged home with instructions on what to expect during her recovery, including potential symptoms such as swelling, sore throat, mild headache, and fatigue.
* She was advised to take her medications as prescribed, avoid driving and excessive head turning, lifting, pushing, or pulling, and to follow a high-fiber diet to maintain strength and assist in wound healing.
* She was instructed to call the office for any changes in vision, slurring of speech, severe headache, or other concerning symptoms.
