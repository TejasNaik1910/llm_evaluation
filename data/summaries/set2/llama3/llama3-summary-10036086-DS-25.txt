Here is a summary of the clinical note:

**Patient Information:**

* 45-year-old male with a history of HIV, prostate cancer, type 2 diabetes mellitus, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, obesity, chronic kidney disease, and asthma.
* Admitted to the hospital with symptoms of tachycardia and dyspnea on exertion.

**Diagnoses:**

* Submassive pulmonary embolism (PE)
* Right lower extremity deep vein thrombosis (DVT)
* Atrial fibrillation (AF) with rapid ventricular response (RVR)
* Acute on chronic heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF)
* Coronary artery disease
* HIV
* Diabetes mellitus II

**Treatment:**

* Anticoagulation therapy with heparin and warfarin
* Cardioversion for AF
* Management of HFpEF with diuretics and vasodilators
* Treatment of DVT with compression stockings and elevation
* Management of HIV with antiretroviral therapy
* Management of diabetes with insulin and oral medications

**Imaging and Diagnostic Tests:**

* Computed tomography angiogram (CTA) of the chest showed pulmonary emboli in the right main pulmonary artery and its branches.
* Transthoracic echocardiogram (TTE) showed mild global biventricular systolic dysfunction and mild to moderate mitral regurgitation.
* Ultrasound of the right leg showed non-occlusive deep vein thrombus.

**Medications:**

* Warfarin 10mg daily
* Amiodarone 200mg PO TID
* Cephalexin 500mg PO Q6H for 4 days
* Enoxaparin sodium 120mg SC BID
* Glargine 60 units breakfast
* Aspirin 81mg PO daily
* Cobicistat 150mg PO daily
* Darunavir 800mg PO daily
* Fenofibrate 145mg PO daily
* Losartan potassium 50mg PO daily
* Metoprolol succinate XL 25mg PO daily
* Odefsey (emtricitabine-rilpivirine-tenofovir alafenamide) 200-25-25mg oral daily
* Rosuvastatin calcium 40mg PO QPM
* Triamcinolone acetonide 0.025% cream 1 appl TP BID

**Follow-up:**

* Patient was discharged home with a plan for follow-up with a cardiologist and primary care physician.
* Patient was instructed to monitor their warfarin levels and adjust their diet accordingly.
